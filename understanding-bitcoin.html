Understanding Bitcoin: The Basics

Bitcoin is a revolutionary digital currency that empowers individuals to control their own money without relying on banks or governments. Created in 2009 by an anonymous person or group known as Satoshi Nakamoto, Bitcoin allows secure, decentralized transactions over the internet.

Key Concepts:

  • Blockchain Technology: The foundation of Bitcoin's security, providing a transparent and immutable ledger.
  • Decentralization: No central authority controls Bitcoin, giving users complete freedom.
  • Limited Supply: Only 21 million Bitcoins will ever exist, making it a scarce and valuable asset.

How Does Bitcoin Work?

Bitcoin transactions are recorded on a public blockchain, which is maintained by a network of computers (nodes). Users can send Bitcoin to one another directly without needing an intermediary. This makes transactions fast, secure, and borderless.
